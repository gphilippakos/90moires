Our Story

A corner on the Laconian Gulf.

Ninety degrees. The corner where our doors meet the harbor road — and the compass bearing our boats take out of Gythio every dawn. Three generations, same angle, same family, since 1959.

The grandfather fished. The son cooked. The grandchildren still do both. What comes out of the water at six in the morning is on a plate by twelve. What doesn't get caught that day, doesn't get served. That's the whole logic of this place.

Our oil is pressed in the Mani hills above us. Our wine is drawn from the barrel. Our fish is priced by the kilo because the fish decides what it weighs, not us. Sit at a table on Tzanni Tzannetaki, order what the day brought in, watch the harbor. That's dinner.

Σήμα Κατατεθέν · The Signature

Grilled Octopus.

the way it's done on the Mani coast

Caught in the gulf, hung on the line to air-cure, then laid on the charcoal. No marinade — just salt, olive oil, oregano, a squeeze of lemon. Tender on the inside, blistered on the outside, and unmistakable. If you only eat one thing at 90 Moires, eat this.

i.

The Catch

Από τη θάλασσα

Sourced fresh from the small boats of Gythio and the broader Laconian gulf. What arrives in the kitchen in the morning is what's on the menu that night — not a day later.

ii.

The Fire

Στα κάρβουνα

Real charcoal, never gas. The octopus spends its time on the wire just long enough for the edges to blister, for the inside to stay soft, for the smoke to find every tentacle.

iii.

The Table

Στο τραπέζι

Served on a simple plate with olive oil, oregano, a wedge of lemon, and a half-kilo of house wine — because the wine and the octopus were made for each other, and everyone in the Mani knows it.
